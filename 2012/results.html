Results and Discussions:

Select graph to view:

We tested Lime extracts on E.coli over 1 hour. The results show that the number of colonies increased during the first 20 minutes before starting to stabilise, showing that it is bacteriostatic.
We also tested our Garlic extracts on E.coli over 1 hour. For the first 20 minutes, it was bactericidal, and after that it started to lose its effect.
These are the results of the test of our Onion extracts on E.coli over 1 hour. For the first 20 minutes, it was bactericidal, showing a drop in the resultant CFU and like Garlic, it started to lose its effect soon after.
These are the results of the test of our Coffee Ground extracts on E.coli over 1 hour. For the first 40 minutes, it was bactericidal, showing a drop in the resultant CFU and like Garlic, it started to lose its effect soon after.
Since both Garlic and Lime extracts worked the best in inhibiting E.coli, we postulate that adding them together would result in a synergistic effect. Therefore, we tested our Garlic-Lime mixtures on E.coli. The results show that the extracts are bacteriostatic for the first 20 minutes, as the resultant CFU does not increase or decrease. After that, it starts to lose its effect.
Tests on other bacteria such as M.luteus show that Onion works better at inhibiting bacteria then Lime. Therefore, we also tested our Garlic-Onion mixtures on E.coli to see if they have a synergistic effect when mixed. The results show that the effect is similar to the results of the Garlic-Lime mixtures. The Garlic-Onion mixture is bactericidal, as the resultant CFU decreased during the first 20 minutes. After that, it became bacteriostatic as the resultant CFU did not have a wide deviation afterwards.
We also tested on M.luteus, which are gram positive bacteria, and see the effectiveness of the food extracts. First, using lime, in a 60 minutes interval, for the first 20 minutes, it had a gradual decrease of bacteria, showing that it is bactericidal. After that, from the 20 minutess to 40 minutess interval, it became bacteriostatic, and manages to suppress the bacteria colonies, until after 40 minutes, the bacteria started increasing slowly and gradually. From this, we are able to see that Lime is able to kill bacteria, up till 40 minutes, where it slowly loses effect.
We also tested on garlic, in the first 20 minutes, the bacteria decreased rapididly, showing its effectiveness in inhibiting bacteria. Then, from 20 minutes to 40 minutes, it starts to lose a little of its effectiveness, but still kills bacteria. From 40 minutes to 60 minutes, it starts to lose effectiveness, but still manages to inhibits growth of bacteria. We can see that the garlic is generally very effective in inhibiting bacteria, and being bacteriostatic in the last few minutes.
Our third food extract, onion, it was bactericidal for the first 20 minutes, and then it slowly loses effect, and the bacteria starts to increase rapidly, and then regains its effectiveness and the bacteria becomes bacteriostatic.
Our last food extract, coffee grounds was bacteriostatic for the first 20 minutes, and inhibiting growth of bacteria. From the 20 to 40 minutes interval, it killed bacteria, resulting the bacteria falling sharply. From the last 20 minutes of the experiment, it started to lose its effect, but was still bactericidal, yet not killing as much bacteria as before.
For the first 20 minutes, it was not effective, resulting in the spike in bacteria growth. Then, it regained its effectiveness and the bacteria decreased gradually. For the last 20 minute interval, it inhibited the growth of bacteria, and the bacteria remained constant. From this we can see that the Garlic-Lime mixture is not effective for the first 20 minutes, and then becomes bactericidal. It slowly loses its effect, but remains bacteriostatic.




Conclusion

After the collection of all these data, we conclude the following:

  • Garlic has the best anti-bacteria properties across a variety of microorganisms
  • Coffee extracts seems to target Gram-positive specific structures
  • Garlic and Lime have antagonistic effects on each other.
  • Garlic and Onion mixtures also have slight antagonistic effects
  • The active compounds in our food extracts are not affected by heat
  • Garlic has demonstrated efficacy in combating bacteria and protozoans
