Methodology

There were many things to do, and many experiments to conduct to get the answers we want. We did 5 main experiments as listed below.

Click on each title to learn more.

1. Preparation of Food Extracts

We decided on 4 foods to test on in this experiment. They are namely, Lime, Garlic, Onion, and Coffee Grounds. These foods have been said to demonstrate anti-bacterial properties.

Below is the main overview of how we went about doing this part of our experiments.

Garlic, Onion and Coffee Beans are blended separately and are made into 50% extracts.

▼

The blended coffee with water is centrifuged. All the extracts are then filtered to extract any solid residue left in them.

▼

The liquefied food extracts are then frozen, for use for experiments later on.

2. Zone of Inhibition (Well of Diffusion)

In order to test out whether each type of food extract actually works, we decided to carry out this experiment.

Below is the main overview of how we went about doing this part of our experiments.

Micro-organisms used: E. Coli, M.luteus and Yeast

▼

The bacterium are placed in centrifuge tubes with Nutrient Broth, and are placed in the orbital shaker for overnight culture. Potato Dextrose Broth is used for Yeast.

▼

The bacterium and Yeast are then swabbed on separate agar plates. Food samples are then introduced on small paper discs, together with a control (sterile water), which are then placed on the agar plates. The plates are then left in the incubator to grow overnight.

▼

The next day, the agar plates are removed from the incubator. The diameter of the zone of inhibition for each of the food sources are then measured and recorded.

▼

The food substance which produced the largest zone of inhibition has the best anti-bacterial properties.

3. Colony Count (Time Point Experiment)

The next step, colony count was done to find out which food source used are bacteriastatic or bacteriacidal. After all, our experiment is to find out which food source not only stops bacteria from reproducing, but also can kill bacteria. The following is how we are going to carry out the experiment:

The micro organisms are adjusted to a concentration of 107 CFU/ml, by means of dilution with nutrient agar

▼

Series dilution is done on the bacteria, with the starting of 0 min, where food extracts are introduced in liquid suspensions. The agar plate is then put into the incubator.

▼

Every 20 minutes, a sample of each mixture is plated and incubated.

▼

After overnight incubation, the plates are removed from the incubator. The resultant colony numbers are then recorded.


Close Section ↑

4. Food Extracts on Water

As a proof of concept, we decided to test the food extracts directly on water samples.

Below shows how we went about doing the experiment.

The water samples (rainwater and tap water) taken before are unfrozen. Using a pipette, a fixed amount of each type of water is then introduced onto 5 agar plates each

▼

Each type of food extract, and with a control (sterile water) are introduced on to 3 different agar plates containing different types of water. They are then mixed with the water.

▼

The samples are then left to grow

▼

After a few days, the plates are removed. The results (amount of bacteria in each plate) are then compared with those done with E.coli, M.luteus
