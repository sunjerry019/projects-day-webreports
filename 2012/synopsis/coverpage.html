Project Title Effects of Common Foods on Bacteria in Water
Synopsis

Over the past decades, outbreaks of water-borne diseases have been increasing, and they continue do so today. Statistics show that water plays an essential role in the transmission of diseases. For instance, in a Pacific Institute Research Report (Gleick, 2002), Diarrheal diseases, infected over 1000,000,000 people that year, and this disease is strongly related to unsanitary excreta disposal and unsafe drinking water.

The objective of this project is to find out the best anti-bacterial common food, namely lime, garlic, onion, and coffee grounds that we encounter in our daily lives, which can kill bacteria found in bacteria in natural water sources, namely rainwater and tap water. M. luteus, E. coli, and yeast will be tested as model organisms. We will be using the zone of inhibition to test out if our food extracts work, and colony counting assays to find out if the food extracts are bactericidal or bacteriostatic. Food extracts that demonstrate efficacy will also be tested on rainwater and tapwater to confirm their use in the field.
