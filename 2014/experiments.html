Procedures

Cultivation of algae

Instant Ocean® was first purchased from a supplier. Tap water was allowed to sit for 3 â€“ 4 days. Seawater was then prepared by adding Instant Ocean® salt to water until the specific gravity of the solution of 1.026 at R.T.P. was obtained by following the recommended concentration. The solution was then left to circulate with a powerhead (pump) overnight before use.

Ulva was then obtained and harvested from a local natural habitat and rinsed in ethanol. Ulva was then submerged in the seawater solution in the tank at a water temperature of 22° C. The tank was then placed 18 to 24 inch (45.72 cm to 60.96 cm) under 200 to 400 foot candles (2152.78 lux to 4305.56 lux) of florescent light. The culture was then allowed to grow.

Preparation of Wastewaters with heavy metal ions

Copper

CuSO4.5H2O + Deionized water

Using calculations, 3 L of stock Copper(II) ion solution was prepared by adding a specific mass of Copper(II) Sulfate pentahydrate salt to deionized water.

Concentrations: 1.3 ppm and 25 ppm

Strontium

SrCl2.6H2O + Deionized water

Using calculations, 3 L of stock Strontium ion solution was prepared by adding a specific mass of Strontium Chloride hexahydrate salt to deionized water.

Concentrations: 13.74 ppm and 960 ppm

Measurement of concentration of strontium

Obtaining a Standard Curve

  1. Set the spectophotrometer to 570nm on absorbance
  2. Prepare a 0.05% w/v xylenol orange solution
    • Measure out 0.252g of xylenol orange and dissolve in 500mL of de-ionized water.
  3. Prepare the strontium test solutions
    • Add 0.765g of SrCl2.6H2O to 250mL of de-ionized water to created a 100ppm solution
    • Perform serial dilution to create a 50ppm, a 25ppm, and a 12.5ppm solution
  4. Fill one cuvette with distilled water, clean with a Kim wipe, and set the absorbance to zero as a blank.
  5. Place 2mL of the xylenol orange solution and 2mL of the strontium test solution into another cuvette.
  6. Wipe with a Kim wipe, run through the spectrophotometer, and record absorbance.
  7. Repeat steps 5 and 6 with each concentration of strontium test solutions, running a blank and re-calibrating to zero after each spectrophotometer use.
  8. Create a standard curve by plotting the absorbance (au) of the test solution over the concentration of the strontium solution (ppm).

The colorimeter was used to test for the final Cu2+ concentration, with necessary dilutions, and spectrophotometer to test for final Sr2+ concentration, also with necessary dilutions. Readings were taken on the fifth day of each trial. We also found the final concentration of heavy metals in the algae, assuming that any drop in heavy metal concentration was resultant of algae bioremediation. To do this, a sample from each of the bottles were taken, and centrifuged, and then the supernatant was tested. The biosorption capacity and biosorption efficiency was then calculated with the following formulae:

Biosorption Capacity

$$q={v(C_i-C)}/{m}$$

where:

$q = \text"biosorption capacity"$ ( $mg$ $g^{-1}$ )
$v = \text"volume of solution"$ ( $ml$ )
$C_i = \text"initial concentration of solution"$ ( $mg$ $g^{-1}$ )
$C = \text"final concentration of solution"$ ( $mg$ $g^{-1}$ )
$m = \text"biomass"$ ( $g$ )

Biosorption Efficiency

$$e={100(C_i-C)}/{C_i}$$

where:

$e = \text"biosorption efficiency"$ ( $%$ )
$C_i = \text"initial concentration of solution"$ ( $mg$ $g^{-1}$ )
$C = \text"final concentration of solution"$ ( $mg$ $g^{-1}$ )
