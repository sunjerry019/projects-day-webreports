Bioremediation of Strontium and Copper in Water by Algae and Sea Urchins

Abstract

protistaproject.weebly.com

Heavy metal pollution is a serious problem that the world is facing today. A remarkable increase in the use of heavy metals over the past few decades has inevitably resulted in an increased flux of metallic substances in the aquatic environment. Algae are a large group of photoautotrophic organisms. They can survive in various kinds of conditions and are extremely easy to grow and can be readily used for bioremediation. In this experiment, dead and live Ulva was exposed to different concentrations of copper and strontium in water. The final concentration of heavy metal ions left in the water was measured after a trial period of five days. Bioremediation capacity and bioremediation efficiency were then calculated. Live Ulva was more effective than dead Ulva in remediating heavy metal ions.

Introduction

With rapid industrialization, there has been a remarkable increase in the use of heavy metals over the past few decades, which resulted in an increased flux of metallic substances in the aquatic environment (Bishnoi, Anju, & Garima, 2004). These metals have a potential to accumulate within biological systems, and if their concentrations exceed allowable limits, it can pose health threats, to not only aquatic animals, but also land animals, including human beings. This project will focus on the bioremediation of non-radioactive Sr2+ and Cu2+ incorporating the use of algae and sea urchins, to explore a more economical method of remediation through the use of biomass.

Objectives

To investigate whether live or dead Ulva are better bioremediators.

This is done through immersing live and dead Ulva in various concentrations of heavy metal ion solutions for five days.

The bioremediation capacity and bioremediation efficiency were then calculated to determine which is the bioremediator.

Hypothesis

Live Ulva will have better bioremediation properties than dead Ulva, due to the live algae being able to assimilate the heavy metal ions into their tissues after taking them in.
